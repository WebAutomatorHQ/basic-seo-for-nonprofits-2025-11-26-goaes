Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Basic SEO for nonprofits (“we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, and protects information when you visit our website, access our resources, or contact us.

By using this website, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please discontinue use of our website and services.

Last updated: November 26, 2025

1. Introduction

We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your information in a transparent and responsible way. This Privacy Policy describes what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have about your data when interacting with the Basic SEO for nonprofits website and related materials.

This policy applies to information collected through:

  • Our public website and landing pages;
  • Contact forms and email communications;
  • Basic analytics tools and third‑party services we use to operate the site.

We do not sell your personal information. We only use your data to provide and improve our content, respond to inquiries, and understand how the website is used so we can make it more helpful for nonprofit teams.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information in two primary ways: information you provide directly to us, and information collected automatically when you use our website.

2.1 Information you provide to us

When you choose to interact with us, you may provide personal information such as:

  • Name and contact details (for example, your email address) when you fill out a contact form or send us an email;
  • Organizational information, such as the name of your nonprofit or your role, if you choose to share it in your message;
  • Any other information you voluntarily provide in the body of your message or form submission.

2.2 Information collected automatically

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect certain technical and usage information, such as:

  • IP address and approximate location (country or region);
  • Browser type, device type, and operating system;
  • Pages viewed, time spent on pages, and referring/exit pages;
  • Date and time of your visit and other diagnostic data.

This information is typically collected through cookies or similar technologies for analytics and security purposes. It is generally processed in an aggregated or pseudonymized form and is not used to identify you personally, unless required for security or legal reasons.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To respond to inquiries: When you contact us, we use your information to reply, provide assistance, or follow up on your questions.
  • To operate and improve the website: We analyze how visitors use the site so we can improve content, usability, and overall experience for nonprofit teams.
  • To communicate with you (if applicable): With your consent, we may send you informational emails or updates related to Basic SEO for nonprofits or related resources. You can opt out of such communications at any time.
  • To maintain security: We may use technical information to monitor for suspicious activity and protect the website and its users.
  • To comply with legal obligations: We may process or disclose information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities.

We process your personal information based on legitimate interests (such as operating and improving the website), your consent (for example, when you submit a form), and compliance with applicable legal obligations.

4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help the site remember your actions and preferences over time and enable certain functionality and analytics.

4.1 Types of cookies we may use

  • Strictly necessary cookies: These cookies are essential for the basic functioning and security of the website and cannot be switched off in our systems.
  • Analytics cookies: These cookies help us understand how visitors use the site (for example, which pages are most frequently visited) so we can improve content and navigation. Data collected is typically aggregated and not used to identify individual users.

4.2 Managing cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings, including blocking or deleting cookies. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality and performance of the website.

For more detailed guidance on managing cookies, refer to your browser’s help documentation or visit publicly available resources about cookie management and privacy controls.

5. Data Sharing and Third‑Party Services

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share limited information with third‑party service providers who help us operate and improve the website, subject to appropriate safeguards.

Examples of such third parties may include:

  • Website hosting providers and technical infrastructure services;
  • Analytics tools that help us understand website usage in an aggregated way;
  • Form processing services that securely deliver your messages to us.

These providers are only permitted to use your information as necessary to perform services on our behalf and are expected to protect it appropriately. We may also disclose information if required by law, to protect our rights, or in connection with a merger or similar organizational change.

6. Data Retention and Security

We retain personal information only for as long as it is reasonably necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy or as required by law. The exact retention period may vary depending on the type of information and the context in which it was provided.

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your information against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or access. However, no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information, including the right to:

  • Request access to the personal information we hold about you;
  • Request correction or update of inaccurate or incomplete information;
  • Request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal obligations;
  • Object to or restrict certain types of processing;
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on your consent.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided in the Contact Information section below. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling certain requests.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection authority if you believe that our processing of your personal information violates applicable law. We would, however, appreciate the chance to address your concerns directly first.

8. International Data Transfers

Our website may be accessed from countries around the world, and some of our service providers may be located in jurisdictions different from your own. As a result, your information may be transferred to, stored in, or processed in countries that may have different data protection laws than those in your jurisdiction. Where required by law, we take appropriate steps to ensure that such transfers are subject to suitable safeguards and that your privacy rights are protected.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, or legal requirements. When we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, provide additional notice.

We encourage you to review this policy periodically so you remain informed about how we protect your information.
